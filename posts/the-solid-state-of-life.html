THE SOLID STATE OF LIFE

May 20, 2026

Sometimes you have to work so hard it stops being discipline and becomes obsession. Not the kind people warn you about. The other kind. The quiet kind — where the problem you fell asleep thinking about is the first thing in your head when you wake up.

I genuinely believe appreciation is overrated. You don't have to be appreciated for the work you do. You don't have to be praised. You don't even have to be heard.

Working hard is an anthem. It plays whether or not anyone is in the room to hear it.

Mathematical models are the true nature of state — that's what I've heard from physicists and scientists. The universe, at its most fundamental level, isn't made of stuff. It's made of relationships. Patterns. Rules that hold regardless of who's watching.

But if nature is really mathematical, what about the solid state of life? Like electrons and particles of state and matter — what about the weight of a decision made at 3 a.m.? The heaviness of working on something no one asked you to, for reasons you can't fully explain?

A rocket engine doesn't ask whether anyone's watching before it fires. The fuel ignites because that's what fuel does when the conditions are right.

You don't have to be heard. You just have to burn.
