# Emergency Management of the Critically Hypoxic, Unconscious Patient

> **Educational clinical reference for trained ER/critical-care clinicians.**
> This is a teaching aid, not a substitute for clinical judgment, ACLS/ATLS/local
> protocols, or senior/specialist input. All drug doses must be cross-checked
> against your local formulary and the patient's weight, allergies, and
> comorbidities before administration. Doses below assume an average **70 kg adult**.

---

## The Scenario

| Parameter | Value | Interpretation |
|-----------|-------|----------------|
| Consciousness | Unconscious | GCS likely ≤8 → **cannot protect airway** |
| SpO₂ | **46%** | Critical, life-threatening hypoxia (peri-arrest) |
| Pulse | 130 bpm | Sinus tachycardia (hypoxic/sympathetic drive) |
| BP | 200/110 | Severe hypertension (hypoxic surge / ↑ICP / primary) |

**Bottom line:** SpO₂ of 46% is incompatible with sustained consciousness — the
unconsciousness is being driven by hypoxia until proven otherwise. This is a
**peri-arrest airway-and-oxygenation emergency**. Fix oxygenation *first*; the
tachycardia and hypertension are usually secondary and often correct once the
patient is oxygenated. **Do not** chase the BP down before fixing the airway.

---

## Step 0 — Activate & Position (0–30 seconds)

1. **Call for help** — resuscitation team / second physician / senior, "Code/Peri-arrest" call.
2. **Position** supine, head of bed flat for airway access (reverse-Trendelenburg/ramped if obese).
3. **Attach full monitoring:** continuous SpO₂, 3- or 5-lead ECG, NIBP cycling every 2–3 min, EtCO₂ if available.
4. **Check a pulse** — if no pulse/central pulse → start **CPR per ACLS** immediately (this protocol assumes a pulse is present).

---

## Step 1 — A: Airway (immediate)

1. **Open the airway:** head-tilt/chin-lift, or **jaw-thrust** if trauma/C-spine concern → apply manual in-line stabilization + collar.
2. **Suction** secretions/vomitus with a **rigid Yankauer** catheter.
3. **Adjuncts:**
   - **Oropharyngeal (Guedel) airway** — size by measuring incisor-to-angle-of-mandible (typically **size 3 = 80 mm** small adult, **4 = 90 mm** medium, **5 = 100 mm** large adult). Only if no gag reflex.
   - **Nasopharyngeal airway** — size **6.0–7.0 mm** (gag intact / clenched jaw); avoid if suspected basal skull #.

---

## Step 2 — B: Breathing & Pre-oxygenation (the priority)

1. **High-flow O₂ immediately:** non-rebreather mask at **15 L/min**.
2. If SpO₂ not rising or patient apneic/hypoventilating → **Bag-Valve-Mask (BVM)** ventilation:
   - **Two-person technique**, two-hand C-E grip, OPA in place.
   - BVM connected to O₂ at **15 L/min** with reservoir.
   - Rate **10–12 breaths/min** (~1 breath every 5–6 s), just enough chest rise (~6–7 mL/kg ≈ 500 mL tidal volume). **Avoid hyperventilation/gastric insufflation.**
3. **Add a PEEP valve** (5 cmH₂O) to the BVM and consider **nasal cannula at 15 L/min underneath** for apneic oxygenation during intubation.
4. **Examine:** chest rise symmetry, breath sounds, trachea, SpO₂ trend.
   - **Absent breath sounds + hyperresonance + hypotension/shock → tension pneumothorax** → immediate **needle decompression** (14G cannula, 2nd ICS mid-clavicular line *or* 4th–5th ICS anterior axillary line) then chest drain.
5. Goal: get SpO₂ up (target **≥94%**, or 88–92% if known CO₂-retainer/COPD) **before** RSI if time allows.

> **Why first:** You cannot safely give induction agents to a patient at SpO₂ 46% — pre-oxygenate/denitrogenate as best you can, even if only for 1–2 minutes of BVM, to buy apneic time.

---

## Step 3 — Definitive Airway: Rapid Sequence Intubation (RSI)

A GCS ≤8 with critical hypoxia needs a **definitive airway**. Prepare with the **7 Ps**.

### Equipment ("SOAP-ME")
- **S**uction (Yankauer, on and working)
- **O**xygen (NRB + nasal cannula for apneic oxygenation)
- **A**irways: **ETT** — **size 7.5 mm** (women) / **8.0 mm** (men) internal diameter; have **0.5 mm above & below** ready. Cuffed.
- **P**ositioning + **P**reparation: ramped/sniffing position; bougie loaded.
- **M**onitors + **M**eds (drawn up, labeled)
- **E**quipment: **laryngoscope** — Macintosh **blade size 3 (small/medium adult)** or **4 (large adult)**; **video laryngoscope** preferred if available. Working light. **10 mL syringe** for cuff. **ETCO₂** detector. **Backup:** supraglottic airway (i-gel **size 4 = 50–90 kg**, **5 = >90 kg**) + surgical airway/cric kit.

### Drugs — RSI

| Drug | Class | Dose (70 kg) | Notes |
|------|-------|--------------|-------|
| **Pre-treatment (optional)** | | | |
| Fentanyl | Opioid | **1–3 mcg/kg = 50–200 mcg** slow IV | Blunts sympathetic/ICP surge; caution if hypotensive |
| **Induction (pick one)** | | | |
| **Ketamine** | Dissociative | **1–2 mg/kg IV = 70–140 mg** | **Preferred here** — maintains BP/airway tone; good if shock. (Theoretical ↑BP — acceptable, oxygenation matters more) |
| Etomidate | Hypnotic | **0.3 mg/kg = ~20 mg** | Hemodynamically neutral alternative |
| Propofol | Hypnotic | **1.5–2.5 mg/kg** | **Avoid** — drops BP (though BP here is high, hypoxic myocardium is fragile) |
| **Paralysis (pick one)** | | | |
| **Rocuronium** | Non-depolarizing | **1.2 mg/kg = ~85 mg** IV | Preferred if hyperkalemia risk; reversible with sugammadex 16 mg/kg |
| Suxamethonium (succinylcholine) | Depolarizing | **1.5 mg/kg = ~100 mg** IV | Fast on/off; **avoid** if hyperkalemia, burns, crush, chronic paralysis |

### RSI Sequence
1. Pre-oxygenate (Step 2).
2. Push **induction** then **immediately paralytic** ("push-push").
3. Wait ~45–60 s (rocuronium) for full relaxation. Maintain apneic O₂.
4. Laryngoscopy → pass ETT (depth at lips ≈ **21 cm women / 23 cm men**, or 3× ETT size).
5. Inflate cuff (minimal occlusion ~ 5–10 mL air).
6. **Confirm:** waveform **EtCO₂** (gold standard), bilateral chest rise, auscultation (axillae + epigastrium), misting, rising SpO₂.
7. **Secure** tube, note depth, **post-intubation CXR** to confirm tip 3–5 cm above carina.

---

## Step 4 — Post-Intubation: Ventilator Settings

Lung-protective volume-control as a safe default:

| Setting | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Mode | Volume Assist-Control (VAC) / SIMV |
| **Tidal volume** | **6–8 mL/kg ideal body weight** (≈ **420–490 mL**) |
| **Respiratory rate** | **12–16 /min** (titrate to EtCO₂/pH; ~14 start) |
| **FiO₂** | Start **100%**, then wean to keep **SpO₂ 94–98%** |
| **PEEP** | **5 cmH₂O** start; ↑ if persistent hypoxia (use ARDS PEEP/FiO₂ table) |
| I:E ratio | 1:2 (longer expiration if obstructive/bronchospasm) |
| Plateau pressure | Keep **< 30 cmH₂O** |
| Target SpO₂ / PaO₂ | 94–98% / 80–100 mmHg (88–92% if COPD) |

- **Sedation/analgesia infusions** post-tube: e.g. **propofol 5–50 mcg/kg/min** ± **fentanyl 25–100 mcg/h**, or **midazolam + fentanyl**; target RASS −2 to −3. (Now that the patient is oxygenated, propofol is fine and will help the hypertension.)
- **ABG** at ~10–15 min to fine-tune.

---

## Step 5 — C: Circulation & the Hypertension/Tachycardia

1. **Two large-bore IV cannulae** — **16G (grey)** or **18G (green)**; consider **IO** if no access.
2. **Bloods on cannulation:** ABG/VBG (+lactate), FBC, U&E, glucose, troponin, coags, group & save, ± toxicology, blood cultures if sepsis.
3. **12-lead ECG** — exclude STEMI, arrhythmia, signs of ↑K⁺.
4. **Reassess BP & HR after oxygenation + sedation** — both usually fall substantially once hypoxia is corrected and the patient is sedated. **Treat the cause, not just the number.**
5. **Persistent severe hypertension** (e.g. >180/120 with end-organ threat, or suspected hypertensive emergency / ICH / aortic dissection):
   - **Labetalol** **10–20 mg IV** bolus, repeat/double q10 min (max ~300 mg), or infusion 0.5–2 mg/min. *(Avoid if asthma/bradycardia/cocaine.)*
   - **Nicardipine** infusion **5 mg/h**, titrate by 2.5 mg/h q5–15 min (max 15 mg/h). Smooth control.
   - **GTN (nitroglycerin)** infusion **5–200 mcg/min** if pulmonary edema/ACS.
   - **Aim:** lower MAP by **no more than ~20–25% in the first hour** (except aortic dissection → faster, target SBP 100–120 + HR <60 with esmolol/labetalol). Over-aggressive lowering risks cerebral/renal/coronary hypoperfusion.
6. **Tachycardia:** treat the driver (hypoxia, pain, anxiety, hypovolemia, sepsis). Rarely needs direct rate control once oxygenated and sedated.

---

## Step 6 — D: Disability / Neuro (find the cause)

1. **GCS** (re-document pre-sedation value), **pupils** (size, symmetry, reactivity).
2. **Capillary blood glucose (CBG)** — *don't ever miss this.* If **<4 mmol/L (70 mg/dL)** → **50 mL of 50% dextrose IV** (or 100–250 mL 10%).
3. **Consider reversible causes / empirical antidotes:**
   - **Opioid toxicity** (pinpoint pupils, RR↓): **Naloxone 0.4–2 mg IV/IM/IN**, titrate. *(Caution: full reversal can unmask agitation/hypertension — small aliquots; airway already secured here.)*
   - **Thiamine 100–250 mg IV** if alcoholism/malnourishment (before/with glucose).
   - Benzodiazepine overdose — **flumazenil generally avoided** (seizure risk).
4. **CT head** once stabilized if unexplained coma / focal signs / ↑ICP / suspected ICH (fits the hypertensive picture).
5. **Signs of raised ICP / herniation:** head up 30°, normocapnia (EtCO₂ ~35–40), brief **hypertonic saline 3% 250 mL** or **mannitol 0.5–1 g/kg**, neurosurgery referral.

---

## Step 7 — E: Exposure & Secondary Survey

1. Full exposure, log-roll, **temperature** (treat hyper/hypothermia), look for rashes (sepsis/anaphylaxis), trauma, fentanyl/drug patches, track marks, medic-alert bracelets.
2. **Collateral history:** family, paramedics, old notes, medication list.
3. Think through the differential of hypoxia + coma + hypertensive-tachycardia:
   - **Pulmonary:** pulmonary edema (cardiogenic/flash), massive **PE**, severe pneumonia/ARDS, asthma/COPD, **pneumothorax**, aspiration, airway obstruction.
   - **Neuro:** intracranial hemorrhage/stroke, status epilepticus, ↑ICP.
   - **Cardiac:** ACS/MI with pulmonary edema, arrhythmia.
   - **Tox/metabolic:** opioids, sedatives, sympathomimetics (cocaine/amphetamine — hypertension+tachy), CO/cyanide poisoning, DKA, sepsis.
4. **Targeted POCUS:** lungs (B-lines, effusion, PTX), heart (RV strain → PE, LV function, effusion), IVC.

---

## Step 8 — Reassess & Definitive Treatment

- **Re-run A→E** after every intervention. Recheck SpO₂, BP, HR, EtCO₂, GCS.
- Treat the **identified cause**: e.g. diuretics/GTN/NIV→tube for pulmonary edema; thrombolysis/anticoagulation for PE; reperfusion for STEMI; antibiotics + fluids for sepsis; BP control + neurosurgery for ICH.
- **Insert urinary catheter** (monitor output), **NG/OG tube** (decompress stomach post-BVM), **arterial line** for beat-to-beat BP if on vasoactive infusions, ± **central line**.

---

## Step 9 — ICU Handover & Transfer

1. **Stabilize before moving** — secured airway, adequate oxygenation/ventilation, BP controlled, infusions running on pumps.
2. **Transfer pack:** full monitoring, transport ventilator (replicate settings), **portable O₂ — calculate cylinder duration** (minute ventilation × FiO₂ factor + reserve), bag-valve-mask + airway/intubation kit, emergency drugs, ambu, suction.
3. **Trained escort** (doctor + nurse), defibrillator.
4. **Structured handover — ISBAR/SBAR:**
   - **I**dentity, **S**ituation: 70 kg adult, found unconscious, SpO₂ 46%, now intubated.
   - **B**ackground: events, PMH, meds, allergies.
   - **A**ssessment: cause (confirmed/working dx), current A–E status, vitals, vent settings, infusions & doses, lines, last ABG.
   - **R**ecommendation: ongoing plan, pending results, anticipated needs.
5. Document everything: times, doses, tube size & depth, response, personnel.

---

## One-Line Mental Model

> **"46% means oxygenate NOW (O₂ → BVM → RSI), secure the airway, then the
> tachycardia and hypertension will mostly look after themselves — meanwhile
> find and treat the cause (DEFG: Don't Ever Forget Glucose), then package for ICU."**
