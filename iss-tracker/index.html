The ISS travels at about 28,000 km/h and circles Earth roughly every 90 minutes — every point on the trail is a moment of human presence in orbit.

Talk to the ISS

There's no internet chat with the crew — but people really do talk to them by amateur (ham) radio. The station carries radio gear through the ARISS program. When it passes overhead, anyone can listen; licensed hams can make contact.

🎙️ Hear the crew (no licence needed)

During a scheduled contact, the astronauts' voices come down on FM at 145.800 MHz. Any handheld scanner or radio — even an SDR dongle — can receive it when the ISS is above your horizon.

📡 Send a message (licensed hams)

The ISS runs an APRS packet digipeater on 145.825 MHz. Licensed operators can send short text messages and have the station relay them. There's also a voice cross-band repeater used between scheduled events.

🏫 Scheduled school contacts

ARISS arranges live Q&A calls between students and astronauts. Schools and groups can apply to host a contact.

📺 Watch right now

NASA streams the station live. Open the NASA Live feed to see Earth from orbit and catch crew broadcasts.
